Symptoms and Signs

If you find yourself or anybody you know experiencing any of the following, or anything similar, either due to social media or not, reaching out to your primary care physician may help you. Let them know what you are feeling and how serious you think it is.

  • Feeling extremely and excessively sad or low
  • Strong feelings of anger or irritability
  • Extreme mood shifts - uncontrollable "highs", feelings of euphoria
  • Having low energy
  • Changes in eating habits
  • Confused thinking; difficult concentrating
  • Overuse of substances such as alcohol and drugs
  • Suicidal thoughts

It is important that you explain all of your symptoms as best as you can. It may be very difficult for you to admit that you need help, but there are people who WANT to help you be healthy and happy. There are many ways to balance social media and your mental well-being.

There are many different types of mental health treatment, and they all do not work the same way for everybody. This is why it is important that you receive unique care for your health. Read more here.

Resources and Organizations

These resources are websites, phone numbers, and places to visit that contain information regarding social media, and its effects on mental health.

    National Suicide Prevention Lifeline (USA)
    • 1-800-273-8255
    • Available 24/7
    National Eating Disorders Association (USA)
    • 1-800-931-2237
    • Available Monday through Thursday 9am-9pm; Friday 9am-5pm (closed on public holidays)
    The Trevor Project (USA)
    • 1-866-488-7386
    • Suicide prevention within LGBTQ+ community
    The Cybersmile Foundation
    • Nonprofit organization dedicated to digital wellbeing and ensuring that bullying and abuse online is dealt with. They focus on awareness, education, and support in order to promote kindness, diversity, and inclusion.
